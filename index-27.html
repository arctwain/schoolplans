Day 121

Reading

  • You'll be reading something a little more classic in the next two weeks, The Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde.
  • Do not rush through this one. Read two chapters a day, and be sure to jot down, look up, and write out a simple version of the definition of any words you don't know.
  • These words will take the place of our words of the day, which will resume when you have read the novel.
  • Who was Oscar Wilde? Born in 1854 in Dublin, Ireland, author, playwright, and poet Oscar Wilde was a popular literary figure in Victorian England during the late 19th century. Known for his brilliant wit and flamboyant style, Wilde wrote many short stories, plays, and poems that continue to inspire millions around the world. His rich and dramatic portrayals of the human condition came during the height of the prosperity that swept through London during that time-- when all citizens of Britain were finally able to embrace literature, that once only the wealthy and educated could afford.

Writing

Pick up any book. It can be something you read recently or even a book you have from when you were younger. Open the book to a random page, read the first full sentence on that page, and use it as the inspiration for a one-page story or scene. Include the original line at the beginning or end of your story.
