Math

  • Read this page about adding fractions, and answer questions 1 - 10 at the end of the page.
  • There are 3 simple steps to add fractions:
    • Step 1: Make sure the bottom numbers (the denominators) are the same; if they are not, change the fractions so that they have the same denominator.
    • Step 2: Add the top numbers (the numerators), and put the answer over the denominator.
    • Step 3: Simplify the fraction (if needed).

History

  • Read pp. 135 - 141 in your history book: The Concise Constitution. Read about the articles, sections, and amendments carefully.
  • The Preable sets out the reasons for a constitution. It states that the people are establishing a new government.
  • This video is still teaching people the Preamble. Give it a try!
  • In your notebook, write down these words and their definitions from the book:
    1. quorum - the required number of people needed to legally do business
    2. posterity - future generations
    3. adjourn - to postpone a meeting to another time
    4. bill - a draft of a law
    5. veto - the power to cancel the actions of another branch of government
    6. override - to have the final authority
    7. pardon - to release or excuse a person from being punished for an offense
    8. impeach - to bring an accusation against a government official
    9. jurisdiction - the authority to administer justice
    10. warrant - the authorization of an officer to make an arrest, seize property, or make a search
  • Remember the main principles of the Constitution:
    • Popular sovereignty
    • Limited government
    • Separation of powers
    • Checks and balances
    • Federalism (the power is divided between the national government and other governmental units)
    • Republicanism (being a citizen in a state as a republic under which the people hold popular sovereignty, which means supreme power or authority)
    • Individual Rights
  • You can memorize these principles and the fact that Rhode Island was the last state to ratify with this mnemonic device (phrase that helps you remember):
    Please, Let's Sign Constitutions Faster, Rhode Island.
