  • Answer the following questions on "Eve's Diary" only:
    1. Who wrote the short story?
    2. Who is Eve?
    3. Who is the "man"?
    4. What does Eve say about feeling like an experiment?
    5. What happens to the moon according to Eve?
    6. What happens to the stars?
    7. Why does the reptile have low taste?
    8. What does it mean when Eve says, "She spoke in quite natural way of pleased surprise"?
    9. What is Eve's fist sorrow?
    10. Why does Eve want to get the apples even though they are forbidden?
    11. What does Eve call "foolishness and a vanity"?
    12. What does Eve say about fear?
    13. For what does Eve say she's made?
    14. What does Eve say will happen by watching?
  • When you're finished, click here to see the answers.
    1. Mark Twain
    2. The main character, a woman who was born yesterday: she considers herself an experiment.
    3. He's also called "reptile", and we can assume he is Adam.
    4. She's convinced she is one, and she's indecisive about whether she is all of the experiment or part of it.
    5. The moon gets loose and falls out of the scheme.
    6. She tries to reach them, but they're farther away than she thought.
    7. Because he is not kind and doesn't do what Eve thinks he should do.
    8. She realized that the animal was a dodo, and she was explaining it.
    9. It was when the man avoided her and seemed to wish she wouldn't talk to him.
    10. Because she wanted to give them to Adam
    11. Her efforts to try to impress Adam
    12. That it’s horrible and ruins her happiness.
    13. She's made for company.
    14. She knows that the stars aren't going to last.
